How it works
The Game of Life is a cellular automaton devised by the British mathematician John Horton Conway. There's no loosing or winning. You can draw inside the circle to fill the cells then just hit "Play" to see them come to life . You can use the various controls to:
Change the colour of the cells

Change the speed of the simulation

or change the zoom

If you're feeling lazy, just click the 'Seed button' and it'll do the work for you randomly filling the cells, then all you have to do is click play to watch them interact.
To randomly colour the cells press the "Rainbow button" .
You can also interact with the cells as they propogate to see how they react to new input. To clear the canvas and start again just click the "Bin button" .
